The Mechanics of the Caesar Cipher

Number of ways a message can be enciphered: 26

Ease of encryption: High

Security: Low

The Caesar Cipher is simple- each letter of a message is mapped to the letter a given number of entries behind it in the alphabet. For instance, the message "Veni vidi vici" enciphered with a shift of two would be "Xgpk xkfk xkek". To decipher a message, one could simply unshift the letters to obtain the original message.
