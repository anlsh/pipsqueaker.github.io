The Mechanics of Nomenclators

Number of ways a message can be enciphered: Infinite

Ease of encryption: Low, almost to the point of unfeasability

Security: High

Nomenclators worked on the same basic principle as monosubstitution ciphers. However, rather than acting upon letters, nomenclators act on words and even phrases. For instance, the message "Attack at dawn" could be enciphered as "λ ζ ψ".

As might be expected, keys for nomenclators were nearly impossible to memorize, and thus nomenclators often necessitated the use of physical dictionaries which were at risk of being captured by the enemy.
