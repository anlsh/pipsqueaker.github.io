The Advent of the Vigenere Cipher

The Vigenere Cipher was actually invented by an Italian named Giovan Battista Bellassa but, as seems to happen so often in history, his invention was completely misattributed to Blaise de Vigenere (pictured).

Nevertheless, the Vigenere Cipher ended the search for a replacement for the Monosubstitution Cipher and would provide near-total security for the next three hundred years.
