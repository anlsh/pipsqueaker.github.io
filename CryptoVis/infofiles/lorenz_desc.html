The Mechanics of the Lorenz SZ40

Number of ways a message can be enciphered: 10^15

Ease of encryption: High

Security: High

The Lorenz SZ40 was a machine both similar to and hugely more secure than the Enigma. Whereas the Enigma used a mere 5 rollers, Lorenz machines used at least 12. Furthermore, rather than basing its encryption on the standard 26-letter alphabet, Lorenz machines used the Baudot Alphabet.

The 32-symbol Baudot Alphabet

The Lorenz machines worked by adding an obscuring letter to each letter of the plaintext. Crucially, the Baudot Alphabet has the property that, if a letter δ is obscured by adding λ via modulo-2 addition to obtain φ, then adding φ to λ will yield δ.

The plain-english way to explain the above is that a Lorenz-enciphered message can be deciphered by adding to the ciphertext the exact same sequence of characters used to encipher it.
