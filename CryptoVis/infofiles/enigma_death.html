The Enigma Exposed

Rejewski, key member of the Polish Cipher Bureau

Cracking the Enigma required almost superhuman ingenuity and perseverance, along with the development of some of the first robots- a much more involved and comprehensive explanation of the process can be found here.

It should be noted, however, that the Enigma was, even with modern technology, essentially impossible to crack. However, German machine certain crucial repetitions, such as broadcasting a weather report each morning. During the 1930's, Marian Rejweski, through a combination of espionage and genius, identified the presence of these repetitions in intercepted messages, allowing Alan Turing to crack the Enigma during WWII.
