The Mechanics of Null Ciphers

Number of ways a message can be enciphered: 26

Ease of encryption: High

Security: Low

Null ciphers are, in fact, a large and diverse group of ciphers. What gives these ciphers their name is the fact that a piece of enciphered text cotains junk, or "null," data.

One example of a null-enciphered message is "dtdlHjkbrdjklrsjklbdrtdhfdrtdjkEbtrljdubjkdrt", which is the phrase "GarbageData" enciphered with a monosubstitution cipher and using d, t, r, j, k, and l as null characters.

Another example would be the ciphertext

Charlie weeded alpine apples smelling terrible
Taking the second letter of each word yields the message "helpme"

The presence of these nulls makes messages,ore difficult to decrypt and, with well-chosen nulls, can even make text enciphered withmonosubstitution-null cipher impervious to frequency analysis. Unfortunately, null ciphers do have a drawback in that they inflate message size which, especially in the modern era of data caps and bandwidth limits, make them unappealing to use.
